Lamborghini Aventador

The Lamborghini Aventador is a mid-engine sports car produced by the Italian automotive manufacturer Lamborghini. Launched on 28 February 2011 at the Geneva Motor Show, five months after its initial unveiling in Sant'Agata Bolognese, the vehicle, internally codenamed LB834, was designed to replace the ten-year-old Murciélago as the flagship model.

Named after a fighting bull that earned the Trofeo de la Peña La Madroñera for its courage in the arena in Zaragoza, Spain, in 1993, the Aventador was designed by Filippo Perini. Powered by a 6.5 L (397 cu in) V12 engine, it produces 730 hp (544 kW; 740 PS) at 8,400 rpm and 509 lb⋅ft (690 N⋅m) of torque at 5,500 rpm. Lamborghini's traditional six-speed manual transmission is offered as an option, but only with the Aventador SVJ model.
